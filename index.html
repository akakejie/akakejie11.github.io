From Playful Kid to Academic Scholar: The Adventures of Nathan Polinar

Childhood

Nathan Polinar was known as the most playful child in his class and neighborhood.1 He was full of energy and loved to have fun, sometimes so much that his parents asked him to calm down. 2 But even though he was playful, he was also emotional and cried easily —whether from happiness or regret. 3 His childhood was full of adventure, laughter, and sometimes tears. 4 One unforgettable moment was when he got electrocuted after accidentally touching a generator while playing tag. 5 Another time, he accidentally hit a friend in the eye with a clay ball, causing him to pass out. 6 That night, Nathan cried nonstop because he felt so guilty.7 At the age of three, he fell in love with basketball.8 Even though it was just a hobby, playing made him happy.9 He began to idolize his dad and grandpa when it came to basketball.10 To gain experience, he started joining leagues with his childhood friends.11 He would wake up early in the morning to practice by himself, eager to improve.12 He also loved to travel.13 One of his favorite memories was a summer trip to Bohol when he was in fifth grade.14 It was his first time riding an airplane.15 He had an amazing time at the beach, sightseeing, and even riding a motorbike.16 At eight years old, a friend introduced him to computer games like League of Legends, Crossfire, and Friv.17 He quickly became hooked and started going to internet cafés every day—even on school days.18 To make time for gaming, he finished his homework during school breaks so he could go straight to the café after class.19 Without realizing it, this was when he started developing an addiction to gaming. 20 He also discovered a passion for dancing while in elementary school.21 He joined an organization that performed during special occasions at his school.22 One memorable moment was when he danced in a Christmas performance, wearing red overalls and a Santa hat.23 Despite being playful, mischievous, and sometimes a troublemaker, Nathan always did well in school.24 He ranked second in his kindergarten class and was consistently an honor student in elementary school.25 His childhood was full of fun and mischief, but it also showed his determination, curiosity, and love for learning.26

Teenage

As Nathan Polinar grew older, he became less playful and more focused on school.1 He graduated from a special section in elementary school and went on to a science high school named Taguig Science High School .2 He was very happy when he passed the entrance exam, but he did not know that high school would be very difficult.3 Even though it was tough, he made new friends who are still close to him today.4 During this time, he had his first crush. 5 He liked a girl and gave her chocolates on Valentine's Day, but she gave them to her friends instead.6 Even though it was disappointing, Nathan did not let it bother him.7 He stayed focused on his studies and continued to get good grades. 8 At 12 years old, he learned how to commute because his school was far from home.9 This gave him the chance to explore new places while traveling for school and fun.10 Around this time, his friends also let him try alcohol for the first time.11 He did not like it at first, but over time, that may have changed. 12 One time, he was so drunk that when he got home at 11 PM, his walk was unsteady.13 However, when he saw his mom, he quickly walked straight to avoid looking drunk in front of her.14 In eighth grade, he injured his right wrist while playing basketball.15 His hand was in a cast for six weeks, so he had to learn how to write, eat, and do other things using his left hand.16 When the COVID-19 pandemic started during his 10th-grade year,he was stuck at home and could not go out.17 Because of this, he ate a lot and gained weight, reaching 75 kg.18 Feeling insecure, he started working out on their rooftop.19 With time and effort, he made progress and gained some muscle.20 During the pandemic, he also bought his first computer, which helped him with online classes and gaming. 21 He met new people online, who helped him feel less lonely during the lockdown.22 It was also through a friend that he met his first and only ex-girlfriend. 23 Even with the challenges in his teenage years, Nathan always found ways to enjoy life.24 He spent time with friends, explored new places,and focused on his hobbies.25 Whether playing basketball, gaming, or just having fun,he made the most of every moment.26

College

Nathan Polinar graduated with honors, he faced a new challenge—college. 1 Questions filled his mind: Which school should I go to? and What course should I pursue? 2 He took the entrance exams for Ateneo de Manila University, the University of the Philippines, and the University of Santo Tomas A, successfully passing all three. 3 However, the more difficult decision was choosing which course to take. 4 Initially, he considered pursuing a business-related degree since he graduated from the ABM (Accountancy, Business, and Management) strand. 5 However, in an unexpected turn, he chose to enroll at the University of the East and pursue a degree in Information Technology . 6 His decision was influenced by his uncle, who had graduated from the same university with the same course. 7 Accompanied by his mother and aunt, he went to the university to complete his enrollment. 8 With that, all that remained was to wait for classes to begin. 9 As the first day of college approached, Nathan experienced a mix of emotions—anxiety, excitement, nervousness, anticipation, and even a bit of fear. 10 The thought of meeting new professors and classmates brought both uncertainty and curiosity. 11 His first day became especially memorable when he mistakenly entered the wrong classroom, ending up in a class for second-year students. 12 It was an embarrassing moment, but one that would later become a funny story to tell. 13 During his first month, he struggled to make friends, as he often sat in the back corner of the classroom—primarily because there was a nearby power socket where he could charge his phone. 14 However, over time, some of his classmates reached out to him, and he gradually formed friendships. 15 Among them were four students from Antipolo, who played a significant role in making his college life more enjoyable. 16 College also introduced Nathan to new experiences, including attending his first party at a club. 17 It was a celebratory gathering with friends to mark the end of a semester. 18 This tradition became something he and his friends looked forward to after the grueling finals week— a well-deserved reward for their hard work. 19 Although Nathan's college journey is still ongoing, he has already created many memorable experiences. 20 Despite enjoying his time in college, he remains committed to his studies, earning a spot as a college scholar.21 He successfully balances academic responsibilities with social activities, proving that dedication and enjoyment can go hand in hand. 22
